From my understanding

  • vec2(x, y) — Creates a 2D point or a vector using the math resource which we used to represent a vertex position on our webgpu canvas. Each corner of our triangles is a vec2.
  • vec3(r, g, b) — According to the math resource is creating our color value (r, g, b). So each corner of the triangle also carries color.
  • mix(u, v, lambda) — loops over the values of two same-length vectors u and v and computes a weighted average using the formula from the math resource So we can use it to blend two positions or two colors. like (r1,g1,b1) and (r2,g2,b2) to get a new color (r,g,b) that is a mix of the two. Or corner_ab is a mix of the two positions for example. Lambda is basically determing how far along we want our resulting point blended between the two inputs so 0.5 is the exact midpoint, less leans toward u, more leans toward v.

1a — What happens if you use a different value for lambda?

Lke mentioed before lambda = 0.5, is the mixed place or midpoint exactly halfway along two given inputs, so it produces three equal sub-triangles at every level. When lambda is changed to any other value, the midpoints are shifted toward one end of each edge. Which makes the sub-triangles unequal in size: one points to the left and the opposite side streches, and vice versa. At lambda = 0 or lambda = 1, all three midpoints collapse to a corner of the triangle, so the sub-triangles get a zero area, and no pattern is produced. So lambda basically controls the balance of the visual appearance by detmeining the weight of the two input vertices in the mix function.

1b — What does the Alpha slider change? How?

The Alpha slider seems to control the transparency of the entire our triangle. Its value (0 to 1) is written into a uniform buffer on the GPU as u_params.x in the resoruc tab. So when we look at the fragment shader, every pixel's output color is used as vec4f(color, alpha) — (r,g,b, and alpah) This is what we control in the slider. The WebGPU pipeline is configured alpha from my research as output.rgb = src.rgb × src.alpha + dst.rgb × (1 - src.alpha) so a value of 1.0 does noting, while going toward 0.0 makes the triangles more transparent by multiplying the color until become invisible against the white background.
